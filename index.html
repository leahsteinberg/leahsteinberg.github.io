Leah Steinberg : Project Dino-Themed Link Shortener

Sun 13 April 2014

It was suggested that we make link shorteners (think bit.ly). We started talking about it. Turns out, there isn't an advanced hashing algorithm involved, like I had assumed there was. My general understanding is that when you give them a long url, they give you a random string, and they save those two together in a database. When you visit your shortened link, they search their database for that short link, and redirect you to its respective long link.

I was underwhelmed. Since their algorithm wasn't that smart, we decided to make ours even goofier. If you head over to lil-dino.com, the world's first dinosaur-themed link shortener, you can give us your unwieldly, uncouth lengthy url. We'll take it, locate a scientifically-valid dinosaur suffix, scrape the page you gave us for an appropriate key word and concatenate the two. For example: www.hackerschool.com becomes lil-dino.com/hackernosaurus. Which, obviously, is longer, but whatever. Here is the code and here is the website. I am as desperate as I've always been for a dinosaur illustration.
